About The Film

The Sound of Amelie is a hand-drawn animated memoir inspired by my experience performing as Louisa in a national Chinese tour of The Sound of Music at age fourteen.

Across 38 cities, I lived between stage lights, hotel rooms, overnight buses, and backstage corridors.

But this is not a story about applause.

It is about the places the audience never sees — the workers dismantling sets at 3 a.m., the loneliness of growing up away from home, friendship, jealousy, first love, and the fragile courage of girlhood.

Through six chapters, the film explores identity, performance, ambition, and what it means to come of age while living inside a world built on spectacle.

THE IMPOSSIBLE DREAM (current) Ch. 01

At a cutthroat Chinese boarding school where academic success determines worth, a bullied fourteen-year-old girl is unexpectedly cast in a year-long national tour of The Sound of Music: it's a dream come true.

Can she choose between the safe path her parents have mapped out and the terrifying freedom of chasing this dream?
